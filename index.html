Muhammad Ali

The Greatest of All Time

Here's a timeline of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942: Cassius Clay is born in Louisville, Kentucky
  • 1960: At 18, Clay defeats Zbigniew Pietrzykowski, of Poland, for an olympic gold medal
  • 1964: At 22, Clay defeats Sonny Liston and becomes the world heavyweight boxing champion
  • 1964: Cassius Clay officially announces he is a Muslim and changes his name to Muhammad Ali
  • 1967: Ali refuses to join the U.S. Army citing his opposition to the Vietnam war. He is barred from boxing and his title is revoked
  • 1970: Ali is reinstated into boxing
  • 1971: "The Fight of the Century" takes place between Ali and Joe Fraizer. This was Ali's first career
  • 1971: U.S. Supreme Court reverses Ali's conviction
  • 1974: Ali and Fraizer have a rematch. Ali wins in 12 rounds
  • 1974: "Rumble in the Jungle" takes place in Zaire. Ali knocks out George Foreman in the eight round using the "rope-a-dope" strategy
  • 1975: Ali fights Fraizer for the third (and final) time. Ali beats Frazier in a 14th round TKO. This fight is now known as "The Thrilla in Manilla"
  • 1978: Ali loses to 1976 Olympic champion Leon Spinks by split decision
  • 1978: In a rematch, Ali defeats Spinks and becomes the first three-time world heavyweight champion
  • 1979: Ali announces his retirement
  • 1980: Ali comes out of retirement to face Larry Holmes. Ali's corner tosses in the towel in the 10th round
  • 1984: Ali is diagnosed by Parkonson's disease, a degenerative disease of the brain
  • 1996: Ali lights the Olympic cauldron during the Atlanta 1996 Olympic games. He receives a replacement gold medal for the one he won in 1960
  • 2016: Ali dies at age 74 in Phoenix, after being hospitalized with respiratory issues
